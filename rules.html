Philosophy & Standards

The Code

We built this city on simple principles: Freedom, Respect, and Common Sense. We don't want to micromanage your fun. Just don't be an asshole.

Community Respect

Zero Tolerance for Hate

This is a game. Real-world bigotry, racism, sexism, or hate speech has absolutely no place here. Play a villain if you want, but don't bring real-world prejudice into our house.

Don't Be Toxic

We’re all here to escape reality and have fun. Leave the OOC drama at the door. If you have a problem with someone, handle it like an adult or open a ticket. Public shaming or toxicity helps no one.

Staff & Tickets

Our staff are humans too. Treat them with respect, and they'll do the same. Use the ticket system for genuine issues, not for spam or trolling.

Gameplay & Freedom

Play Your Way

We believe in FreeRP. You can be in Discord with your friends. You can meta-game a little if it makes the story better for everyone (just don't use it to win). As long as you aren't ruining someone else's experience or gaining an unfair competitive advantage, go for it.

Value Life & Roleplay First

Even in a free environment, the story comes first. If a gun is pointed at your head, act like it. If a cop pulls you over, engage with them. Don't just treat this like a TDM lobby.

Government Corruption

Corruption is allowed but handled completely In-Character. If you get caught, you face the in-city consequences (jail, firing, etc.). There are no admin punishments for corruption unless you are griefing the entire server (e.g., deleting all database records).

Conflict & Consent

Combat Etiquette

Don't just RDM (Random Death Match). Give people a reason and a chance to react before shooting. Conflicts should have a story behind them.

Graphic Content & ERP

We're all adults here (18+ server). Graphic themes and ERP are allowed ONLY with explicit consent from all parties involved. If someone says stop or seems uncomfortable, you stop immediately. No exceptions.

New Life Rule

If you are fully executed (dead-dead), you forget the specific details of your death. You can remember the "vibe" or general tension, but you can't go back for immediate revenge knowing exactly who shot you.

Rules are subject to common sense. If you're trying to find a loophole, you're probably breaking the spirit of the rule.
